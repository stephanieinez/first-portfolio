Hector Berlioz: Symphonie fantastique op. 14 H 48
Daniele Gatti, Koninklijk Concertgebouworkest
PREMIUM
 
The release of this live recording celebrates the inauguration of Daniele Gatti as the Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra's new chief conductor in September 2016. You can hear the intensity and joy of making music together in this recording. It gives a good idea why the members of the Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra chose Daniele Gatti as their new chief conductor.

Robert Schumann: Szenen aus Goethes Faust (Scenes from Goethe's Faust) WoO 3
Daniel Harding, Symphonieorchester des Bayerischen Rundfunks, Chor des Bayerischen Rundfunks, Christian Gerhaher (Baritone), Christiane Karg (Soprano), Mari Eriksmoen (Soprano), Alastair Miles (Bass) u.a.
PREMIUM
 
Robert Schumann’s “Szenen aus Goethe’s Faust” have been persistently underrepresented in the 20th century concert life, although being considered one of Schumann’s masterpieces: initially planned as an opera and finally realised more in an oratorio form, there is hardly another of his works Schumann has engaged himself in for such a long time.
The baritone Christian Gerhaher has a big stake in the fact that this work has attracted more attention again in the past years; his profound interpretations of the title role have drawn new attention to the Faust Scenes. Experience him in this recording, partnered by Christiane Karg as Gretchen, with Daniel Harding conducting the Symphonieorchester des Bayerischen Rundfunks.

Ludwig van Beethoven: Fantasia for Piano, Choir and Orchestra in C minor op. 80 “Choral Fantasy”
Karl Böhm, Wiener Symphoniker, Chor der Wiener Staatsoper, Hans Richter-Haaser (Piano), Teresa Stich-Randall (Soprano), Judith Hellwig (Alto), Hilde Rössel-Majdan (Alto), Anton Dermota (Tenor), Erich Majkut (Tenor), Paul Schöffler (Bass-baritone)
 
The “Choral Fantasy” premiered on December 22, 1808 and was conducted by Beethoven himself, in a 4-hour concert marathon at the Theater an der Wien in Vienna. It did not only feature the public premiere of the Choral Fantasy but also of Beethoven's Symphonies 5 & 6, and his Piano Concerto No. 4. Beethoven specifically composed the Choral Fantasy to grandiosely close this concert by brilliantly uniting the solo piano, orchestra and chorus. This recording features pianist Hans Richter-Haaser, the Wiener Philharmoniker and the Chor der Wiener Staatsoper, under the baton of great Karl Böhm.

Cellomania
 
When thinking about the cello as a solo instrument, you might first think of the famous compositions by Vivaldi, Bach, Boccherini, Schumann or Tchaikovsky. However, it was during the 20th century that the most cello compositions were written. Here, we have collated some 20th century works for the cello, played by some of the best cellists of our time.

Maria Callas: Lyric and Coloratura Arias
 
Shortly after having finished the recording sessions for her famous Puccini recital in 1954, Maria Callas returned to the studio for another recital recording. While the first part of the recital presents a number of lyric arias from verismo operas, the second part is a firework of coloratura arias by Italian and French composers.
